flection

Thoughts gathered over time. Things I'm learning, unlearning, or still trying to understand.

May 2025

On Slowness

I've been trying to do fewer things — and do them more completely. The urge to fill every moment is a kind of fear, I think. Slowness isn't laziness. It's trust that there will be enough time for what matters.

This year I started leaving an hour in each day with no plan. It's harder than it sounds.

January 2025

What Reading Teaches

Every book is a temporary loan of another consciousness. You inhabit a different nervous system for a few hours. When it's good, you return to your own life slightly rearranged.

I finished Marilynne Robinson's Gilead this winter and felt — for the first time in a long while — genuinely moved by prose.

September 2024

On Making Things

The fear of making something bad is almost always worse than the thing itself. Most of what I've made has been imperfect. A small amount has surprised me. I'm learning that the ratio is fine.
